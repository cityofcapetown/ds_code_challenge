# City of Cape Town - Data Science Unit Code Challenge

## Technical Assessment: Head of Data Product Services
________________________________________
### Overview
The City of Cape Town is on a mission to become a more responsive, efficient, and data-driven organisation. The Head: Data Product Services role is central to transforming the City’s data assets into reliable, scalable, and user-centric analytic and AI products.
________________________________________
### The Scenario: “City Pulse”
The City’s current system for handling citizen service requests is fragmented and inefficient. Residents reporting issues such as potholes, leaks, or faulty streetlights must navigate cumbersome web portals or wait in long queues for our call center. The data associated with these incidents is siloed across departments (Water, Electricity, etc.), preventing a unified view of service delivery challenges and confounding opportunities for proactive maintenance.

The Data Analytics Hub has been tasked with leading the development of a new data product, City Pulse, to address this challenge.

A key strategic requirement from the Mayor’s Office is to leverage WhatsApp as the primary, user-facing channel for reporting and communication, given its near-universal adoption among residents. The goal is to make reporting an issue as simple as sending a message to a friend
Your task is to define a practical strategy and roadmap for delivering this product.
________________________________________
### Tasks:
1. **Product Vision & User Definition**
Product Vision Statement: Write a single, inspiring sentence describing the long-term impact City Pulse will have on the City and its residents.

* Identify three key user types (e.g., Residents, Senior City Management, Ward councillors, City Depot managers). Select one of these user types and describe: 
  * Their primary goal when using the current system
  * Their pain points with the current system
________________________________________
2. **Strategy & Success Metrics**
Core Strategy: Outline your core product strategy. How will City Pulse succeed where others have failed? What is its unique value proposition?

Objectives and Key Results (OKRs): Define one critical objective for the first 12 months of City Pulse, supported by 3-4 key measurable results (specific and quantifiable).
________________________________________
3. **The User Journey & MVP**
WhatsApp User Journey: Describe the step-by-step experience for a resident reporting a pothole through WhatsApp. Detail the interaction from the user’s initial message to the final confirmation that the issue has been resolved.

Include: 
* What information will be requested from the user (text, photo, location)?
* How will the Bot respond to users and guide them?
* How will users be kept informed?

MVP Feature Set & Prioritisation: List 5-7 essential features for a minimal viable product (MVP). Present your prioritisation using a simple Value vs. Effort matrix (or similar). Describe how you would use this to prioritise features.
________________________________________
4. **Data & Technical Considerations**
Data Ecosystem: Identify key data sources City Pulse must integrate with (e.g., GIS, departmental ticketing systems).

* Future Value of Data: Describe one advanced analytical use case for City Pulse (e.g., predictive analytics, resource optimization), relevant to the user type you selected in Task 1.
* Risks & Mitigation: List two major data or technical risks to the project and how you would mitigate each.
________________________________________
5. **Implementation & Governance Plan**
Describe how you will ensure that City Pulse aligns with the City’s data product governance and management standards.

Include how you would handle the following: 
* Metadata management and documentation practices 
* Quality assurance and validation processes 
* Cross-departmental data ownership and accountability 
* Governance frameworks or standards you would apply (e.g., DAMA, FAIR)
  
Then, assume limited integration capacity and uneven data quality across departments. Explain how you would phase feature delivery to ensure early, wins while building toward the fully featured product.

Include:
* an annotated data product architecture diagram (wireframe/flow diagram/ mockup/etc.) and 
* a governance workflow that helps communicate your governance or implementation approach.

In 150 words or less, describe how you used (or why you did not use) AI tools in completing this assessment, and what limitations you encountered.
